# Delivery Model Analysis
## Spiritual Gifts Assessment - Productization Strategy

*Created: December 24, 2025*

---

## Overview

This document analyzes five potential delivery models for commercializing the Spiritual Gifts Assessment platform. Each model has different tradeoffs for revenue, scalability, and operational complexity.

---

## 5 Delivery Models

### 1. SaaS Multi-Tenant ‚≠ê **Recommended**
**You host everything, churches pay subscription**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Recurring revenue | You handle all ops/support |
| Easy updates for all users | Need hosting budget upfront |
| Low barrier to entry for clients | Multi-tenant complexity |

**Pricing Model**: $29-99/month per church based on size  
**Implementation Effort**: High  
**Revenue Potential**: High (recurring)

---

### 2. White-Label License
**Churches host themselves with your branding removed**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| One-time or annual license fee | Less recurring revenue |
| No hosting costs for you | Support complexity |
| Scales with minimal effort | Version fragmentation |

**Pricing Model**: $500-2000 one-time + $200/year maintenance  
**Implementation Effort**: Medium  
**Revenue Potential**: Medium (one-time)

---

### 3. Managed Hosting (Premium)
**You deploy dedicated instances per church**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Higher price point | More ops overhead |
| Complete customization | Doesn't scale as easily |
| Premium support included | Higher hosting costs |

**Pricing Model**: $199-499/month per dedicated instance  
**Implementation Effort**: Medium  
**Revenue Potential**: High (premium pricing)

---

### 4. Open Source + Commercial Support
**Free core, paid enterprise features**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Builds community & trust | Revenue comes slower |
| SEO & organic discovery | Competitors can fork |
| Enterprise upsell opportunity | Support expectations |

**Pricing Model**: Free core + $99-299/month for enterprise (SSO, analytics, priority support)  
**Implementation Effort**: Medium  
**Revenue Potential**: Variable (long-term)

---

### 5. Church Management System Integration
**Plugin/integration for existing platforms**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Access to established user base | Platform dependency |
| No hosting needed | Revenue sharing |
| Easier sales through marketplace | API constraints |

**Target Platforms**: Planning Center, Breeze, Church Community Builder, Tithe.ly  
**Implementation Effort**: Medium  
**Revenue Potential**: Medium (revenue share)

## Decision Framework

### Choose **SaaS Multi-Tenant** if:
- You want predictable recurring revenue
- You can invest in infrastructure and support
- You want to control the user experience
- You're targeting many small-to-medium churches

### Choose **White-Label License** if:
- You want quick cash flow with less ongoing work
- Your clients have technical capability to self-host
- You don't want ops responsibility

### Choose **Managed Hosting** if:
- You're targeting large churches or denominations
- You can provide premium support
- Clients need extensive customization

### Choose **Open Source** if:
- You want to build community and trust first
- You're playing a long game
- You can upsell enterprise features later

### Choose **Platform Integration** if:
- You want access to existing customer bases
- You don't want to do sales/marketing
- You're okay with revenue sharing

---

## Recommendation

**Start with SaaS Multi-Tenant** for these reasons:

1. **Recurring Revenue**: Monthly subscriptions provide predictable income
2. **Control**: You own the customer relationship and data
3. **Updates**: Ship features once, all customers benefit
4. **Scalability**: Multi-tenancy allows efficient growth
5. **Market Validation**: Easy to pilot with a few churches before scaling
